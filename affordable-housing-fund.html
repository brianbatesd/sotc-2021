Affordable Housing Fund

Vancouver voters approved creation of the seven-year, $42 million Affordable Housing Fund (AHF) in 2016. This property tax levy is capped at $6 million per year. The revenue is used to buy, build and preserve low-income rental housing and support rental assistance and housing programs that help prevent or address homelessness.

How Was the Revenue Spent in 2021?*

Housing Production: $3,875,000 (70 new units)

Housing Preservation: $700,000 (18 existing units)

Homelessness Prevention: $1,547,190 (310 households stabilized)

Temporary Shelter: $402,527 (45 beds ongoing operations)

Administration: $278,401

*The revenue spent came from taxes collected in 2021 and in previous years.

Project highlights

The Residences at Arnada (1600 E. Street) will be completed in early 2022 and will provide 12 affordable units within the 83-unit development.

Neals Lane (2609 E. Neals Lane) was completed in 2021 and created 9 affordable housing units in partnership with Ginn Group.
