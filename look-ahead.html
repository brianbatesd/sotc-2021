Look Ahead

Looking ahead to 2022 and beyond, the City will work alongside our community to help shape the future. As Vancouver continues to grow and community needs and priorities change, the City is working to develop a new six-year strategic plan. Over the course of the next year, there will be continued opportunities through this process and other avenues to share your thoughts and ideas about our evolving community.

Hereâ€™s a look of some of the exciting projects and initiatives planned for 2022:

Improving public safety

  • In February, voters approved a property tax increase (Proposition 2) to pay for hiring additional firefighters, equipment and construction or seismic retrofitting of fire stations to improve emergency response times, including:
    • Five fire captains, 11 fire medics, 24 firefighters, and support staff
    • One new squad apparatus and ladder truck
    • Replacement of Fire Station 3 (1110 N. Devine Rd.) and Fire Station 6 (3216 N.E. 112th Ave.)
    • Seismic improvements, expansion or renovation of Fire Station 4 (6701 N.E. 147th Ave.), Fire Station 5 (7110 N.E. 63rd St.) and Fire Station 8 (213 N.E. 120th Ave.)
  • Implementing a comprehensive body-worn, dash and in-car police camera program later this year.
  • Recruiting for both experienced and entry-level police officers and offering hiring bonuses to help give us a competitive edge.
  • Conducting a nationwide search for the next Vancouver Police Chief to replace retiring Chief James McElvain.
  • Fire Station 11 will open in the Orchards/Sifton area of Clark County this fall to improve our public safety infrastructure and emergency response for more than a quarter of a million residents currently served by the Vancouver Fire Department.

Addressing climate change

  • Adopting the City’s first climate action plan to become carbon neutral citywide by 2040.

Responding to homelessness

  • Establishing two additional supportive Safe Stay Communities to support up to 80 unsheltered residents with safe housing and services to transition out of homelessness.
  • Recruiting and hiring a new homeless outreach coordinator to provide outreach and referrals to appropriate services or individuals experiencing homelessness.
  • Evaluating locations to establish a second Safe Parking Zone for community members living in their vehicles.

Improving livability

  • Completing playground remodels at three parks, including a space-themed playground at Rose Village and accessible, inclusive Harper’s Playground designs at Esther Short and Marshall parks.
  • Major private and public developments permitted by the City are slated to open, including:
    • AC Marriott Hotel at Terminal One on the Vancouver Waterfront
    • Hotel Indigo and Condos at the Vancouver Waterfront
    • Holland Headquarters and 110 apartments at Block 10 in downtown Vancouver
    • Vancouver Innovation, Technology and Arts (VITA) Elementary School in downtown Vancouver
    • New Mountain View High School
    • WXV, 69-units of affordable housing for seniors on 15th and Mill Plain
    • Aegis Phase 1 mixed-use housing and office development giving the historic Providence Academy site in downtown Vancouver new life
    • Vancouver Landing at the Port of Vancouver’s Terminal 1, including pier and trail rebuilding on the west side of the waterfront.

Enhancing City services

  • Upgrading the City’s utility payment system to a modern, easier-to-use bill payment portal for Vancouver utility customers.

Investing in underserved business districts

  • Receiving $32.6 million dollars from the federal government to support local pandemic recovery through the American Rescue Plan Act. The City will work alongside the community on how a portion of these dollars will support investments in the Fourth Plain multicultural district in central Vancouver, and the Main Street Corridor downtown.
